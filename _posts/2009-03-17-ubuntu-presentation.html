Overview: Ever heard of complaints about Microsoft Vista being slow as hell? Ever had thoughts of switching operating systems. Ever had a thoughts of adopting linux? I have found my answer to those questions in UBUNTU(translation for humanity to others). Ubuntu is a Linux based operating system that came around in 2004. Since it's original release Ubuntu had at least several releases already.

Reasons for switching:

1- Need for Innovations, and NEW!
As a computer engineering major, I was desperate of something new and classy, I was desperate of something that would differ from windows and allow me a the freedom to dominate and control my OS.

2- Need for something Free (with no asterisk)
Ubuntu, like most linux distributions is an open source meaning completely free. I needed something that would cost nothing at all.

3- Eager to learn
I was eager to explore, and started a "crusade of knowledge" (one of my fetish clauses). I wanted to learn.

Why Ubuntu Specifically: Someone on one of the blogs I read often told me that I could have acquired the same experience in any linux distribution. so Why Ubuntu?

a- THE COMMUNITY
right before starting the process of learning Ubuntu I was facing a little challenge. I wanted to know how I could get help when needed? After going through tons of websites, I realized one thing. Ubuntu has the biggest support community with thousands of posts that meet ur needs.

b- THE INTERFACE / SPEED
before choosing ubuntu, i also looked at Fedora, OpenSuse, PcLinusOS, and Kubuntu. There are different development environments, or if you like, looks for linux. 2 of the most popular ones are: kde and gnome(ubuntu runs on gnome). To be honnest Kubuntu has been my favourite choice, but unfortunately kde is bulky, and a little bit slow for me. It looked so appealing with its crazy plasma themes, but i needed something fast and efficient. I loved also both OpenSuse and Fedora, but couldn't find enough support for my system.

c-THE BEST
before installing linux, i did my research, and i wanted to know, which linux OS exactly was the most userfriendly, and easy to manipulate, and the answer overwhelmingly was Ubuntu.
